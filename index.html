Trekking Equipment List

What We Provide

  • Wind & waterproof Dome Tent (2-3 pax)
  • Warm Sleeping Bags & Thick Mattresses
  • Complete cooking & dining utensils
  • Private emergency toilet tent
  • Standard First Aid Kit (P3K)

What You Should Bring

  • Windbreaker jacket & thermal fleece
  • Trekking shoes (non-slip) & thick socks
  • Headlamp or good flashlight
  • Gloves, beanie, and extra clothes
  • Sunblock cream, sunglasses, & personal meds

Safety Procedures

Your safety is our ultimate priority. Trekking Mount Rinjani carries natural risks, but with careful preparation and an experienced local team, we minimize those risks so you can enjoy the hike.

Certified Guides

All our guides hold official certifications from the National Park and are trained in handling Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) and basic mountain first aid.

Emergency Comms

Our team is equipped with two-way radios (HT) to monitor weather changes and communicate directly with basecamp and national park rescue teams.

Extra Logistics

We always pack emergency backup food and drinking water beyond the planned itinerary to prevent shortages in case of unexpected bad weather.

Evacuation SOP

We have clear, tested evacuation protocols in coordination with the National Park authorities should a hiker get injured or experience extreme exhaustion.
