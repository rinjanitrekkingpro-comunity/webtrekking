Safety Procedures

Your safety is our top priority. Trekking Mount Rinjani carries risks, but with careful preparation and the right team, these risks can be minimized.

Certified Guides

All our guides are trained in basic mountain first aid and hold certifications from the National Park. They know how to handle acute mountain sickness (AMS).

Emergency Communication

Our team is equipped with two-way radios (HT) to monitor the weather and communicate with the basecamp or rescue teams in case of an emergency.

Sufficient Logistics

We always bring backup food and water beyond what is planned to prevent running out of supplies due to bad weather.

Evacuation SOP

There are clear evacuation procedures in case a hiker gets injured or experiences extreme fatigue, working closely with National Park authorities.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can beginners hike Mount Rinjani?

Yes, but physical preparation is required at least 1 month before the hike, such as regular jogging. We highly recommend the 4D3N or 3D2N packages for beginners as the pace is more relaxed.

Which month is the best to hike?

The dry season between April and November is the best time. The National Park is typically closed from January to March due to extreme monsoon rains.

Do porters carry my personal backpack?

The porters we provide are responsible for carrying camping equipment and food logistics. If you need an extra porter for your personal belongings (clothes, etc.), they can be hired for an additional fee.
