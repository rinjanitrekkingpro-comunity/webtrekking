Mawun Beach Lombok: A Hidden Tropical Paradise in the South

Are you looking for the ultimate tropical escape? While Lombok is globally celebrated for its diverse shorelines, there is one secluded spot that consistently steals the hearts of travelers seeking absolute peace: Mawun Beach.

Tucked away on the southern coast of Lombok, Mawun Beach is renowned for its powdery white sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and a beautifully tranquil atmosphere. If you want to escape the bustling crowds and experience untouched nature, this horseshoe-shaped bay needs to be on your travel bucket list.

The Allure of Mawun Beach

What makes Mawun Beach so visually striking is its unique geography. The beach forms a nearly perfect half-moon bay, flanked by two rugged, lush green hills that act as natural wave breakers. This creates a calm, lagoon-like environment in the center of the bay, making it incredibly safe and relaxing for swimming.

Because it remains somewhat off the mainstream radar, you won't find rows of noisy beach clubs here. Instead, you are greeted by an expansive coastline facing the Indian Ocean, offering uninterrupted serenity.

Getting There & Entrance Fees

  • From Mataram City: It takes about 2 hours via the Batu Jai route. You can easily combine this trip with a stop at the famous Selong Belanak Beach.
  • From Lombok International Airport: It is a quick 25-minute drive via the Kuta Mandalika route.
  • Entry Fee: Access is incredibly budget-friendly, costing around IDR 5,000 for scooters and IDR 10,000 for cars.

Top Things to Do at the Bay

  • Swim and Stroll: The central waters are calm and inviting. The pristine white sand is perfect for barefoot beachcombing.
  • Sip Fresh Coconuts: Support the local vendors dotted along the tree line. Rent a simple sun lounger and enjoy a freshly cut coconut under the shade.
  • Surf the Edges: While the center is calm, the outer edges of the bay near the hills catch the Indian Ocean swells, providing great breaks for surfers.
