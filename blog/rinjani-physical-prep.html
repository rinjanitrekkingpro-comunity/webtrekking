5 Essential Physical Preparations Before Summiting Mount Rinjani

10 March 2026 | Admin Rinjani Pro

Standing tall at 3,726 meters above sea level, Mount Rinjani offers one of the most breathtaking volcanic landscapes in Southeast Asia. However, the journey to the summit is incredibly demanding. The steep inclines, loose volcanic scree, and long hours of walking require solid stamina.

To ensure you enjoy the trek safely and comfortably, here are 5 simple yet essential physical preparations you should start at least a month before your hiking day.

1. Build Your Cardiovascular Endurance

Rinjani’s trails will test your lungs. Build your stamina by doing cardiovascular exercises 3 to 4 times a week. Running, brisk walking, cycling, or swimming for at least 30 to 45 minutes a session will significantly improve your body's oxygen efficiency at high altitudes.

2. Strengthen Your Leg and Core Muscles

Your legs will carry you up, but your core will keep you balanced. Incorporate strength training into your routine. Focus on squats, lunges, and calf raises to build lower-body power. Add planks and sit-ups to strengthen your core, which is vital for carrying your daypack steadily over uneven terrain.

Squat exercises are very helpful to conquer the hills of regret.

3. Practice Hiking with a Weighted Backpack

The best way to train for a hike is to actually hike. Find local hills or stairs and climb them while wearing a backpack loaded with 5 to 7 kilograms of gear. This perfectly simulates the conditions of the Mount Rinjani trekking routes and gets your shoulders accustomed to the weight.

4. Focus on Flexibility

Stiff muscles are more prone to injury and cramping. Spend 10 to 15 minutes stretching after every workout. Yoga is highly recommended for trekkers as it improves both flexibility and breathing control—two crucial elements for surviving the Rinjani summit attack.

5. Mental Preparation and Acclimatization

Trekking Mount Rinjani is 50% physical and 50% mental. There will be moments of exhaustion, especially during the final push through the volcanic sand in the dark. Mentally prepare yourself for the challenge and trust the process.
