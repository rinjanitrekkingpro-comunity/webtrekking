Everything You Need to Know About Mount Rinjani

March 30, 2026 | Rinjani Trekking Pro

Timing and logistics are everything when planning a major expedition. If Mount Rinjani is on your bucket list, here is the complete breakdown of weather patterns, permits, and booking details to secure your spot.

Trekking Season and Weather

Mount Rinjani National Park is closed annually from January to March due to heavy monsoon rains and to allow the ecosystem to recover. The 2026 trekking season officially opens on April 1st!

  • April to May: The landscape is incredibly lush and green right after the rainy season. This is a highly recommended time to witness the savannahs at their best.
  • June to August: The peak season. Expect dry weather, clear skies, and spectacular sunrise views, though the trails can become quite dusty.
  • September to December: Still great for trekking, but expect occasional afternoon showers as the wet season approaches.

Clear skies over the crater rim during the dry season.

Permits and Quotas

To preserve the pristine environment, the National Park strictly limits the number of daily visitors. This means permits sell out very quickly, especially during the peak months of July and August. Booking your trek well in advance is highly recommended to secure your preferred dates.
