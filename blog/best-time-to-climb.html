When is the Best Time to Climb Mount Rinjani in 2026?

Published: March 2026 4 min read

Trekking Mount Rinjani is a bucket-list adventure for many hikers worldwide. Standing at 3,726 meters above sea level, it is the second-highest volcano in Indonesia. However, conquering this majestic peak requires careful planning, especially when it comes to the weather.

1. The Dry Season: April to November (The Best Time)

The absolute best time to climb Mount Rinjani is during the Indonesian dry season, which typically runs from April to November. During these months, the weather is more predictable, the trails are safer (less slippery), and you have the highest chance of experiencing clear, stunning views of the Segara Anak Lake and the summit sunrise.

  • April - May (Shoulder Season): The landscape is incredibly lush and green right after the rainy season. It's a beautiful time to hike, though you might still experience occasional light afternoon showers.
  • June - August (Peak Season): This is the most popular time to hike. The weather is dry and sunny, but the trails can get crowded. Be prepared for very cold temperatures at the summit during July and August, sometimes dropping near freezing.
  • September - November: The crowds start to thin out, and the weather remains mostly dry. It’s an excellent window for a quieter trekking experience.

2. The Wet Season & Park Closure: January to March

You cannot climb Mount Rinjani from January to March. Every year, the Mount Rinjani National Park authority strictly closes all trekking routes during these months.

Why is the park closed?

The closure serves two critical purposes: Safety and Ecosystem Recovery. The monsoon season brings heavy rains, making the steep trails incredibly slippery, prone to landslides, and highly dangerous. Additionally, the closure gives the flora and fauna of the national park a much-needed break to recover from human activity.

3. How Cold Does It Get?

Don't let the tropical Indonesian climate fool you. While it might be 30°C (86°F) at the beaches of Lombok, the temperature at the Rinjani Crater Rim at night usually drops to around 5°C to 10°C. If you are doing the summit push in the early hours of the morning, wind chill can make it feel closer to 0°C (32°F).

Pro Tip: Always pack a high-quality windbreaker, thermal base layers, a beanie, and warm gloves. (Don't worry, if you book with Rinjani Trekking Pro, we provide thick, warm sleeping bags for your comfort!)
